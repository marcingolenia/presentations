Pair programming myths and facts

Myth #1 It will double the cost

Counter-fact #1 It saves time and money

Counter-fact #1 It saves time and money



source: The Costs and Benefits of Pair Programming - Alistair Cockburn, Laurie Williams.
What we can learn from Laurie William's experiment is that in the first experiment the students needed extra time to get used to each other - the time cost was greater. However in the next iterations the extra time cost settled at about 15% extra value. Without any further investigation we can say that yes, pair programming costs time. But Laurie decided to investigate further and learned that there is more than 15 percents defects reduction rate in all of the 3 experiments. Investment.

#4 - Forcing people to do pair-programming 🙁

not everyone can pair or will want to pair. Pairing might not work for someone who is toointroverted, soft-spoken, or lacks confidence. Perhaps this person can "grow out" of that with a nonthreateningpairing with a fabulous mentor or with another introverted, soft-spoken person. We must, however, continue to respect the work of pairs and solo people
